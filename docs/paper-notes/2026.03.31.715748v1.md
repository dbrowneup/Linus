# Generative machine learning unlocks the first proteome-wide image of human cells

## TL;DR

ProtiCelli is a conditional latent-diffusion **Diffusion Transformer Large (DiT-L)** model that generates 512×512
single-cell immunofluorescence images for **12,800 human proteins** from just three landmark stains — nucleus (DAPI),
endoplasmic reticulum, and microtubules — across **39 cell lines**. Trained on **1.23 million single-cell crops from the
Human Protein Atlas** under the EDM (Elucidating Diffusion Models) framework, with a Stable Diffusion 3.5 VAE encoding
into a 16×64×64 latent space and learnable per-protein and per-cell-line embeddings as conditioning. It outperforms the
prior state of the art (PUPS, CellDiff) across pixel-level reconstruction (Pearson, mutual information), textural
fidelity (Haralick, MS-SSIM), and overall image realism (FID 6.46 vs CellDiff's 36.58 and PUPS's 134.08). Generalization
is non-trivial: ProtiCelli predicts drug-induced changes in protein expression and localization for Paclitaxel and
Vorinostat-treated MDA-MB-468 cells from morphology alone, infers cell-cycle stage without dedicated cycle markers (a
small fine-tune closes the gap), and supports unsupervised subcellular-compartment segmentation. The big artifact is
**Proteome2Cell** — 30.7 million simulated images forming 2,400 "virtual cells" across 12 cell lines, integrated into
HPA v26 for interactive exploration. Limitations: protein conditioning is through learnable embeddings (no protein
language model), so it cannot generalize to unseen proteins; the model is trained on healthy cell lines under standard
conditions; vesicular compartments (peroxisomes, endosomes, lysosomes) remain hard. The paper also ships a clean
LLM-agent integration surface (`agent_tools.py`, JSON-Schema tools with explicit Anthropic/OpenAI adapters) — an unusual
and welcome inclusion. This is the **imaging-FM specialist** for a specialists-as-Workers Linus.

## The problem (in plain language)

Cell biology runs on the spatial organization of proteins: where a protein sits inside a cell often determines what it
does, and roughly one-sixth of pathogenic coding mutations work by mislocalizing a protein. The Human Protein Atlas has
spent two decades systematically imaging human proteins one at a time using immunofluorescence (IF) microscopy, with a
small set of reference markers per image to anchor cellular landmarks. The result is a beautiful catalog of subcellular
localization — but at one protein per image. Multiplexed spatial proteomics methods can run ~37 markers in the same
field of view, but a human cell contains thousands of proteins, and protein localization varies from cell to cell, and
changes with cell state and perturbation. Building "every protein, every cell, every condition" experimentally is
several orders of magnitude beyond what any wet-lab can deliver.

The natural question: can a generative model trained on the HPA learn the spatial syntax of the human proteome well
enough to **synthesize** the missing images? Two prior models had tried — PUPS (a UNet with a protein language model and
an image-inpainting head) and CellDiff (a unified diffusion model that bidirectionally maps protein sequence to image).
Both produce systematically biased outputs (over-smoothed, biased toward nuclear/cytosolic localization), both
undershoot textural fidelity, and neither has generated a quantitative spatial-proteomics dataset that other biologists
can actually query. The field also lacked a shared evaluation framework — most virtual-staining papers benchmark on
proxy metrics rather than on biological tasks downstream cell biologists care about (co-localization, drug-response,
cell-cycle inference, segmentation).

ProtiCelli sets out to close both gaps: train a stronger generative substrate, and use it to generate a proteome-scale
synthetic dataset that supports biologically meaningful downstream queries.

## What they propose

**Architecture.** A latent-space conditional diffusion model. Inputs are 512×512 four-channel images (microtubules,
protein, nucleus, ER) at the HPA's pixel size of **0.1067 µm/px**. A frozen Stable Diffusion 3.5 VAE encodes 4-channel
pixel space into a **16×64×64 latent** representation; single-channel protein images are replicated to RGB before
encoding. The denoising model is a **DiT-L** (Diffusion Transformer Large): 28 transformer blocks, 16 attention heads,
head dimension 72, total embedding dimension D=1152, processing 32×32 patches in the 64×64 latent with patch size 2×2.
Conditioning combines learnable protein embeddings (12,800 entries) and cell-line embeddings (39 entries) with timestep
features through six adaptive-normalization parameters per block (shift, scale, gate for both attention and FFN paths).
Cell-line dropout (probability 0.5) trains a default unconditioned embedding for novel lines.

**Training.** EDM preconditioning (σ_min=0.002, σ_max=80, ρ=7), AdamW (lr=1e-4, weight decay 0.01) with linear warmup.
50 denoising steps at inference. The MT (microtubule) channel doubles as the intensity-normalization anchor: clip at the
99.5th percentile of MT, then divide all channels by that value, then rescale to [-1, 1]. This single choice — using a
structural channel as the global photometric reference — propagates through the preprocessing pipeline
(`ImageNormalizer` in the released library) and is the kind of opinionated default Linus tools should preserve when
wrapping the model.

**Datasets.** HPA v24 (12,808 proteins × 39 cell lines, 1.23M single-cell crops, 1.15M train / 0.08M test) for
pretraining. CM4AI (112,410 cells from MDA-MB-468 treated with Paclitaxel, Vorinostat, or vehicle) for drug-
perturbation generalization. A bespoke FUCCI-CellCycle dataset (A-549 and MDA-MB-468 stained for GMNN + CDT1) for
cell-cycle prediction. A 3D z-stack dataset for organelle segmentation.

**Proteome2Cell.** The headline downstream artifact: 30.7 million ProtiCelli-generated images forming **2,400 "virtual
cells"** (200 per cell line × 12 cell lines), each containing the predicted localization patterns of all 12,800
proteins. Integrated into HPA v26 (releasing September 2026) for interactive exploration. Each virtual cell is a
queryable substrate for spatial-proteomics analyses that would be experimentally impossible.

**Downstream tasks.** Each demonstrated as a use-case rather than an isolated benchmark: (1) virtual staining and
held-out test-set evaluation; (2) cell-line-specific localization (e.g. EGFR localizing to Golgi in SiHa cells but
plasma membrane in A-431 — only ProtiCelli gets this right); (3) drug-perturbation prediction from morphology alone (no
protein channel); (4) cell-cycle stage inference (zero-shot mediocre, fine-tuned strong); (5) hierarchical clustering of
subcellular compartments using SubCell embeddings of generated images; (6) protein-assembly visualization; (7) gene-set
spatial enrichment (e.g. spatially decomposing the Reactome "Metabolism of Lipids" pathway into ER vs lipid-droplet vs
mitochondrion sub-pathways); (8) unsupervised segmentation of subcellular compartments via spatial clustering of
organelle-marker generations.

**Released.** Model weights, the `proticelli` Python package (PyPI-style with `pip install -e .`), preprocessing
pipeline (`ChannelAssembler`, `ImageNormalizer`, `ResolutionResampler`), and an LLM-agent tool registry
(`agent_tools.py` exposing `validate_inputs`, `predict_from_files`, `search_proteins`, `list_cell_lines` with both
Anthropic and OpenAI adapter one-liners in the README). The Proteome2Cell dataset is integrated into the HPA web
interface rather than distributed as a downloadable bulk resource.

## Key results

- **Image quality vs prior art.** FID 6.46 (ProtiCelli) vs 36.58 (CellDiff) vs 134.08 (PUPS). Pearson R > 0.65 across
  most nuclear and perinuclear structures. Haralick textural metrics and MS-SSIM clearly above both prior baselines. All
  three models fail similarly on vesicles (peroxisomes, endosomes, lysosomes) — the spatial relationship between
  vesicles and the three landmark channels is intrinsically weak, not a model-architecture failure.
- **Cell-line-specific localization.** EGFR Golgi vs plasma-membrane vs mixed patterns recovered correctly across SiHa,
  A-431, HeLa. PUPS and CellDiff uniformly default to plasma membrane.
- **Drug-perturbation transfer.** On the CM4AI MDA-MB-468 dataset (untreated / Paclitaxel / Vorinostat), ProtiCelli's
  predicted intensity changes correlate with experimentally measured changes for proteins where it has cell-line
  context, demonstrating that the model has learned drug-induced morphology shifts despite never seeing CM4AI data
  during training.
- **Cell-cycle inference.** Zero-shot the model is mediocre on FUCCI cell-cycle markers; a small fine-tune (the same
  `model.fit` pathway exposed in the public API) lifts performance to actionable. The authors frame this as the
  "lab-in-the-loop" pattern — predictions guide which experiments to run, those experiments retrain the model.
- **Hierarchical subcellular maps.** Leiden clustering on SubCell embeddings of generated images recovers GO-Cellular
  Component categories (nucleus, cytosol, mitochondrion, ER, etc.) at progressively finer resolution. Proteome2Cell maps
  (200 cells × 12,800 proteins per cell line) recapitulate real-image hierarchical structure.
- **Pathway spatial decomposition.** Reactome "Metabolism of Lipids" (R-HSA-556833, 758 proteins, 566 in vocabulary)
  spatially decomposes into ER-anchored, lipid-droplet-anchored, mitochondrion-anchored sub-pathways with one-sided
  Wilcoxon FDR < 0.05 in Hep-G2 cells. This is a bona fide systems-biology output from a generative model.
- **Unsupervised segmentation.** Spatial clustering of generated organelle markers gives subcellular masks (nucleoli,
  nuclear speckles, nucleoplasm, ER, etc.) that quantify protein localization with greater precision than whole- cytosol
  measurements — without any organelle-segmentation ground truth.
- **Protein-assembly visualization.** Generated co-images of known protein assemblies (e.g. ribosome, mitochondrial
  import complex) preserve known co-localization.

## What's reusable in Linus

This paper maps to Linus along three concrete fault lines.

**Phase 7 — imaging-FM specialist Worker (the headline use).** Per the
[biological-foundation-models synthesis](../syntheses/biological-foundation-models-synthesis.md) specialists-as-Workers
framing, ProtiCelli is the imaging-microscopy specialist that pairs with Bacformer (whole- genome representations) and
Evo 2 (genomic FM) — three different bio FMs covering three different modalities, each deployed as a Worker behind a
typed-output Linus tool. The artifact ProtiCelli produces (a 12,800-protein synthetic IF stack for a single cell,
conditioned on cellular landmarks) is exactly the shape of output Phase 7 wants from a biology Worker: bounded inputs,
interpretable outputs, queryable downstream. Concrete tool sketch:
`linus.skills.proticelli.predict_subcellular_localization(landmarks: ImageStack, proteins: list[str], cell_line: str | None) → list[GeneratedImage]`.
Wraps the released `Model` class plus `ChannelAssembler` / `ImageNormalizer` / `ResolutionResampler` preprocessing,
returning typed structured prediction ([CLAUDE.md typed-structured-prediction convention](../../CLAUDE.md)) — a
`SubcellularLocalizationPrediction` record that includes the generated image, predicted-organelle distribution,
confidence, evidence (prompt landmarks + model checkpoint hash), and free-text rationale.

**`model_prediction` edge class (DEC-0048) — direct hook.** Each ProtiCelli prediction is a `model_prediction` edge into
the KnowledgeBase. The required fields land cleanly: protein-id node + cell-line-id node as endpoints, model
identifier + checkpoint hash + prompt landmarks as provenance, predicted localization-distribution + confidence as the
edge payload. Per DEC-0048 the edge inherits the `[!unverified]` claim-type tag from DEC-0023 by default; Dan upgrades
verified predictions (e.g., experimentally confirmed Paclitaxel-induced relocalization) explicitly. Proteome2Cell would
be a 30.7M-edge bulk import — a real stress-test of the schema in a way the smaller-scale Evo 2 or Bacformer outputs
would not provide.

**`external_api_tool` deployment field (DEC-0046) — operational hook.** ProtiCelli's `agent_tools.py` exposes
`PROTICELLI_TOOLS` in standard JSON Schema with explicit Anthropic and OpenAI adapter examples in the README. This is
already shaped like a Linus tool registry entry. The DEC-0046 deployment field decides whether ProtiCelli runs as an
in-process Worker (local inference) or as an external HTTP/MCP service (remote inference); given M1 Max constraints (see
hype filter below), a hybrid pattern — local for one-off predictions, remote/batch for Proteome2Cell-scale synthesis —
is the realistic deployment.

**Phase 4 — HPA v26 + Proteome2Cell mirroring.** Once HPA v26 ships (Sep 2026) with Proteome2Cell integrated, the Data
Sovereignty pillar can mirror the synthetic dataset alongside the real HPA images for offline access. 30.7M images is
large but tractable on the 1 TB external SSD if Linus mirrors a per-cell-line subset. Cleanly scoped: even if Linus
never re-trains ProtiCelli, having Proteome2Cell + HPA v26 locally enables RAG over predicted localizations for any
protein-of-interest query Dan does in the course of his LanzaTech metagenomics work or his B. braunii residual research
interests.

**Phase 6 — fine-tuning lab-in-the-loop.** The `model.fit` pathway is fully exposed in the public API. The
cell-cycle-marker fine-tune (FUCCI-CellCycle, ~5,000 cells, 50 epochs) is the kind of LoRA-scale workload that plausibly
fits on M1 Max in mixed precision. Demonstrating Linus can drive a ProtiCelli fine-tune end-to-end on a small custom
dataset would be a satisfying Phase 6 demo, independent of language-model fine-tuning — and would exercise the
`[fine-tune]` Worker pattern without committing to the much larger Evo 2 or Kimi-K2 surfaces.

**Phase 3 — protein-batch agent.** ProtiCelli inference parallelizes trivially across proteins (each prediction
independent, batch_size parameter exposed). A `protein-batch` agent fanning a list of proteins-of-interest out to
ProtiCelli inference is the imaging analogue of the `variant-batch` agent flagged in the
[Evo 2 paper-note](s41586-026-10176-5.md). Both demonstrate Phase 3 Worker fan-out on Dan-relevant biology.

## What's NOT applicable / hype filter

**Local-inference operational reality.** ProtiCelli is a DiT-L (~458M parameters, FP32 = ~1.8 GB just for weights; plus
the SD 3.5 VAE which is another ~250 MB; plus EDM activations across 50 denoising steps at 16×64×64×16 latent batch).
The default device fallback is `"cuda"` then `"cpu"`. There is no MLX port. PyTorch MPS support for the
diffusion-transformer + VAE combination is plausible but unverified — and the EDM sampling loop in `_sampling.py` wants
to be benchmarked against MPS before any Phase 7 commitment. Realistic local path: 7B-Evo-2-style — load the unet and
VAE in fp16, batch_size=1 for proteome-scale, accept ~tens of seconds per image instead of GPU-class sub-second. For
Proteome2Cell-scale workloads (30.7M images) the answer is unambiguously "remote endpoint or batch job," not local M1
Max.

**Protein vocabulary is closed.** ProtiCelli encodes proteins through learnable embeddings, not through a protein
language model. **It cannot generalize to proteins outside the 12,800-vocabulary.** This is the most operationally
important limitation: if Dan wants to predict subcellular localization for a metagenomic novel protein, ProtiCelli will
silently fall back to a default embedding (or refuse, depending on which API surface is hit). The authors note this as a
future-work direction — incorporating ESM-style features would enable open-vocabulary prediction. Until then, ProtiCelli
is a closed-world model on the human proteome.

**Cell-line vocabulary is also closed (with graceful fallback).** Only 39 cell lines are conditioned. Novel cell lines
fall back to a default unconditioned embedding (tested via the `label_dropout_prob=0.5` training regime). This is more
forgiving than the protein limitation but still a hard ceiling.

**DEC-0047 biosecurity tier — appropriately scoped out.** The [SAFETY.md three-tier policy](../../SAFETY.md) Tier B
applies to **generative biology** in the sense of designing sequences, organisms, or molecules. ProtiCelli generates
**microscopy images** of human proteins given landmark stains — it is not synthesizing DNA, not designing proteins, and
not operating in the dual-use generative-biology space [Sandbrink et al. 2306.03809](2306.03809v1.md) warned about.
Properly scoped, ProtiCelli predictions stay at **Tier A** (standard sandbox). The framework still applies in the sense
that any future ProtiCelli-derived workflow that crosses into sequence/structure design (e.g., using predicted
localization changes to suggest engineered mutations) would graduate to Tier B; the gating is "does the output cross a
synthesis or design boundary?", not "does it touch biology?".

**Vesicular compartments remain hard.** All three tested models — ProtiCelli, CellDiff, PUPS — fail similarly on
peroxisomes, endosomes, and lysosomes. The authors attribute this to weak spatial correlation between these vesicles and
the three landmark channels (nucleus / ER / microtubules). For Linus this is a hype filter on any "ProtiCelli predicts
every organelle" framing — vesicular biology is structurally outside the model's reach without additional landmark
stains.

**Health-bias.** Trained exclusively on standard cell lines under normal conditions. The CM4AI drug-perturbation
generalization is a positive result but not a guarantee that more extreme perturbations (CRISPR knockouts, infection,
disease tissue) would transfer. Phase 7 deployment should treat ProtiCelli predictions outside this distribution as
**`[!unverified]` claims with low priors** in DEC-0048 terms.

**Diffusion-Transformer lineage.** DiT-L is the architecture from Peebles & Xie 2023 (the "Scalable Diffusion Models
with Transformers" paper that anchored the SD3 / SD 3.5 / FLUX line). EDM (Karras et al. 2022) is the diffusion
training/sampling framework that proved more numerically stable than vanilla DDPM. Latent-space diffusion via the SD 3.5
VAE is the same recipe Stability AI uses for image generation. ProtiCelli is therefore not a bespoke architecture — it's
the modern open diffusion stack ported to immunofluorescence microscopy, with bio-specific conditioning and
normalization choices on top. The portability of those base components is a positive sign for local-deployment effort.

**LLM-agent integration.** The release ships `agent_tools.py` with explicit Anthropic and OpenAI adapter one-liners in
the README. This is unusually MCP-friendly for a biology-paper release — most bio releases stop at a CLI or a notebook.
Per [DEC-0045](../adr/0045-fastmcp-mcp-framework-default.md) (fastmcp as the default MCP framework), the Linus tool
wrapping ProtiCelli should follow the in-house fastmcp + middleware pattern from
[g6-mcp-tools](../syntheses/repo-clusters/g6-mcp-tools.md) — using `agent_tools.py` as the schema reference rather than
vendoring the helper directly.

**Lab-in-the-loop.** The cell-cycle-marker fine-tune (mediocre zero-shot, strong after a small fine-tune)
operationalizes a pattern Linus should support natively: prediction generates a hypothesis, a targeted experiment
refines the model, the next prediction is better. This connects to the agentic-systems thread on
inference-time-compute-via-fine-tuning and the Phase 6 fine-tuning lane (DEC-0014).

## Open questions for Dan

1. **Is ProtiCelli the right Phase 7 imaging-FM specialist, or wait for an open-vocabulary successor?** The
   closed-protein-vocabulary limitation is the load-bearing one. ProtiCelli works beautifully on the 12,800 proteins it
   was trained on, but fails open on novel proteins (metagenomic discoveries, viral proteins, engineered constructs). A
   version with ESM-conditioned protein embeddings (which the authors flag as future work) would be strictly more useful
   for your LanzaTech metagenomics workflow. Lock ProtiCelli in for Phase 7 now, or watch for the v2 release first?

2. **MPS viability — has anyone tested it?** The repo's `Model` class auto-falls-back to `"cpu"` if CUDA isn't
   available. PyTorch MPS support for DiT-L + SD 3.5 VAE + EDM 50-step sampling is plausible but unverified. Worth a
   focused spike: load the released checkpoints in fp16 on M1 Max, run a single prediction, log wall-clock + memory. If
   the answer is "minutes per image, but it works," ProtiCelli is a Phase 7 local Worker; if "memory blow-up" or
   "completely broken," it shifts to the `external_api_tool` deployment field with hosted inference (Stanford ELL vault,
   HPA web interface, or a cloud endpoint) as the primary path.

3. **Proteome2Cell mirror in Phase 4?** 30.7M images is on the order of low-hundreds of GB on disk, tractable on the 1
   TB external SSD. Mirroring a per-cell-line subset (e.g., HeLa + U2OS + A-431 + HEK293, which covers most
   reference-cell-line biology) is much smaller. Worth the disk and the bandwidth, or wait until HPA v26 ships the web
   interface and call it remotely?

4. **ProtiCelli + Bacformer + Evo 2 + TranscriptFormer + biophysics PLM as the Phase 7 specialist panel?** The
   biological-foundation-models synthesis specialists-as-Workers framing wants three to five strong domain models, each
   behind a typed Linus tool. Confirming the panel: Evo 2 (DNA), LucaOne (DNA + RNA + protein), Bacformer
   (whole-bacterial genomes), the biophysics PLM (proteins), TranscriptFormer (transcriptomics), ProtiCelli (imaging).
   Five-modality coverage. Is that the right Phase 7 ambition, or scope down to two or three for the MVP?

5. **`model_prediction` edge stress-test with Proteome2Cell.** Per DEC-0048, the `model_prediction` edge class needs to
   land in the KB schema before any Phase 7 biology Worker writes to it. Proteome2Cell would be the largest single bulk
   import — 30.7M edges, all `[!unverified]` by default, with a well-defined provenance chain. Worth using as the
   stress-test corpus for the schema, or start smaller (e.g., a per-cell-line subset, or Bacformer's protein-cluster
   embeddings)?

6. **Lab-in-the-loop as a Phase 6 demo workload?** The cell-cycle-marker fine-tune (~5,000 cells, 50 epochs, fully
   exposed via `model.fit`) is plausibly tractable on M1 Max in mixed precision. Demonstrating that Linus can drive a
   ProtiCelli fine-tune end-to-end — call `model.fit` with a small custom dataset, save the new checkpoint, run
   predictions through the same `predict()` API — would be a satisfying Phase 6 demonstrator that doesn't depend on any
   LLM fine-tuning being ready. Worth scoping as a parallel-to-LoRA Phase 6 demo, or strictly out of scope?
