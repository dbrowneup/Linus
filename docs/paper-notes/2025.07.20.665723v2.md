# A contextualised protein language model reveals the functional syntax of bacterial evolution

## TL;DR

Bacformer is a 12-layer transformer (hidden 480, ~tens of M params above frozen ESM-2 35M) that treats a bacterial
genome as an **ordered sequence of proteins** and learns the genomic syntax linking them. Each protein is embedded with
frozen ESM-2, augmented with rotary positional embeddings and learnable contig tokens, and the protein-token sequence is
fed through the genome-scale transformer in one pass. Pretraining is BERT-style masked prediction over a 50,000-class
protein-family vocabulary built by k-means clustering 12.3M ESM-2 embeddings; corpus is **1.3M metagenome-assembled
genomes (MAGs) over ~25,000 species, ~225M contigs, ~3B proteins** (5.6 TB embeddings), extended with 47,832 complete
RefSeq genomes. The pretrained model clusters strains by phylogeny unsupervised (kNN balanced accuracy ~0.95 at genus),
and after fine-tuning predicts: operons (AUROC 0.76–0.92 across five species, validated by the authors' own long-read
RNA-seq), STRING PPIs, gene essentiality (median AUROC 0.81, beating fine-tuned ESM-2 and Evo), KEGG protein function
(188 classes), and 139 phenotypic traits. The same architecture generates novel, non-memorised bacterial genomes
conditioned on a property token (oxygen requirement, optimal temperature) with matching functional enrichment. Code and
weights are open on GitHub and HuggingFace under Apache 2.0.

## The problem (in plain language)

Bacteria run most of the planet's biogeochemistry, and their genomes encode that diversity in a highly structured way:
functionally related genes cluster into operons, transcription factors regulate proximal targets, and gene identity
correlates with distance from the origin of replication. The dominant computational approach has nonetheless been to
look at proteins one at a time — annotate each independently with a sequence-based PLM (ESM-2, ProtT5), or apply
task-specific pipelines for BGCs, AMR, or essentiality. The genomic _context_ that gives a gene its functional meaning
is largely thrown away.

Existing genome-scale attempts either operate on raw DNA (DNABERT, Nucleotide Transformer, Evo) — spending most of their
capacity learning to identify coding regions before they can reason about function — or on small windows of neighbouring
proteins (gLM, SAFPred). Neither scales to whole genomes across the bacterial kingdom. The authors' wager is that the
right unit is the protein but the right **token** is the protein-as-element-of-a-genome: the same protein in two
different genomic contexts should get two different embeddings, because its functional role does in fact differ. They
build the model that operationalises this and show it beats per-protein embeddings on the things microbiologists care
about — operons, essentiality, function, phenotype.

The Dan-relevant subtext: this is the bacterial-genomics counterpart to what the broader Group A wave (Evo 2, LucaOne,
AlphaGenome, RiNALMo, ProteinReasoner, METL, REMME/REBEAN) is doing for nucleotide and protein modalities individually.
Bacformer claims the _whole-genome bacterial_ slot, with metagenome-scale training and an explicit eye toward microbial
ecology and phenotype inference.

## What they propose

The architecture is conceptually simple. Each genome G = (P₁, …, P_N) is an ordered list of proteins (by contig
coordinate). Each protein-token is the sum of (1) length-averaged ESM-2 residue embeddings, (2) a learnable contig
embedding shared across all proteins on the same replicon (chromosome vs plasmid distinguishable), and (3) rotary
positional embeddings inside attention encoding protein-to-protein distance. [CLS], [SEP], [END] delimit boundaries.
Crucially, the protein-family pretraining vocabulary is _constructed_, not curated: k-means (k = 50,000) over 12.3M
ESM-2 embeddings via CuPy, validated against eggNOG (NMI 0.74) and MMseqs2 (NMI 0.88). This dodges the closed-vocabulary
problem limiting DNA LMs.

Training is HPC-class: 16 Intel Ponte Vecchio GPUs × ~30 days to embed 1.3M genomes (5.6 TB output), then up to 1.2M
steps on 4 A100s (~2 weeks) with effective batch 32, peak LR 1.5e-4/GPU, Adam, FlashAttention. Two variants released:
autoregressive next-family generator and BERT-style masked predictor. Downstream fine-tuning typically freezes the
encoder and trains a small cross-entropy head.

Evaluation is broad: strain clustering vs ESM-2 mean-pooling, operon prediction vs Operon DB and the authors' own
long-read RNA-seq on five species, STRING PPI, gene essentiality on the DEG database (51 genomes / 37 species, beats
fine-tuned Evo and ESM-2), KEGG-L4 function over 188 classes (503k held-out proteins), and 139 phenotype traits from
Madin et al., Traitar, and Brbić et al. The generative head produces de novo genomes conditioned on a phenotype token
prepended to the prompt.

## Key results

Genome-level mean-pooled Bacformer embeddings recover phylogeny unsupervised: kNN balanced accuracy ~0.95 at genus,
~0.85 at species, with Bacteroidales cosine-similarity heatmaps showing within-genus 0.96 ± 0.02 and within-family 0.93
± 0.05. On _E. coli_ MGnify isolates Bacformer reveals six Leiden subpopulations invisible to ESM-2 alone, with
cluster-specific accessory genes (_cbtA_, _stxA_, _fldP_) — biologically meaningful subspecies structure discovered
without labels.

Operon prediction is where the contextual hypothesis pays off most cleanly. Bacformer's pairwise contextual cosine
similarity beats ESM-2 across all operon sizes against Operon DB, and on the authors' own long-read RNA-seq of five
species (_M. tuberculosis_, _M. abscessus_, _S. aureus_, _P. aeruginosa_, _E. coli_) zero-shot AUROC ranges 0.76–0.92.
STRING-DB PPI prediction is strong zero-shot and improves significantly with fine-tuning (p < 0.01); novel _P.
aeruginosa_ PAO1 PPIs validated with AlphaFold3 give significantly higher ipTM scores than random pairs (p < 0.003).

Gene essentiality is the headline cross-method comparison: fine-tuned Bacformer reaches median AUROC 0.81 ± 0.07 across
10 held-out species and beats fine-tuned ESM-2 _and_ fine-tuned Evo (the major DNA LM in this space). Many "false
positives" are genes with known-essential orthologs elsewhere (ribosomal proteins, tRNA synthetases) — biologically
plausible mistakes. KEGG-L4 protein function over 188 classes (210k train / 503k test proteins) shows Bacformer sustains
accuracy in low-data regimes and clusters embeddings cohesively (ASW 0.71 vs. -0.05 for ESM-2); even with the query
protein masked, Bacformer hits 0.56 balanced accuracy from context alone.

Phenotype prediction trains separate heads on frozen genome embeddings for 139 traits over ~24k annotated genomes, beats
ESM-2 and a no-pretraining ablation, and is then used to annotate the entire 1.3M MAG corpus across 32 high-confidence
traits — released as a public resource. Gradient attribution surfaces canonical sporulation regulators (_spoIIIAG_,
_spoIIIAF_, _sigG_, _spoIIAB_) as top scorers and identifies host-specific adaptations in _Lactobacillus johnsonii_
(_lacA_/_lacR_ in human, _cas9_ in livestock, _banIM_ in chicken).

The generative results are the ones Dan should weigh most carefully. Prompted with ~500 initial proteins from held-out
species, Bacformer produces protein-family sequences at perplexity ~1.03–1.05 with realistic functional-category
distributions and **no exact replicas of training genomes** under temperature sampling. Genome completion recovers the
full true genome at MRR > 0.6 with 50% of proteins missing. Conditioning on a phenotype token yields matching functional
enrichment — aerobic prompts enrich _pucL_ and cytochrome-c, deplete _narL_ and _hycA_; psychrophilic prompts enrich
cold-active _xynB_ and β-galactosidase _bgaB_. Output is at the _protein-family_ level; a contrastive fine-tuning step
enables specific protein-sequence retrieval from generated embeddings.

## What's reusable in Linus

**Phase 7 (Skills & Autonomy) — the obvious one:** "Bacterial genome analysis" is exactly the kind of domain-specific
skill the Phase 7 graduation is designed to host. Operon prediction, gene essentiality, KEGG-function annotation, and
139-trait phenotype inference are all callable as zero-shot or fine-tuned heads on a single open-weights model with
HuggingFace checkpoints under Apache 2.0. A Linus tool wrapping `bacformer.predict_operons(genome_path)` or
`bacformer.infer_phenotypes(genome_path)` fits cleanly into the orchestration-layer tool registry and gives Dan a
private, local genome-analysis capability hosted Claude does not have.

**Phase 6 (Fine-Tuning) — viability assessment:** The Bacformer transformer is small (12 layers, hidden 480, ~tens of M
params on top of frozen ESM-2 35M) — well under 100M total. LoRA on the M1 Max is comfortably tractable; full
fine-tuning is plausibly tractable. The expensive piece is the embedding step (16 Ponte Vecchio GPUs × 30 days for 1.3M
genomes), but for any single Dan-relevant genome (a few thousand proteins) ESM-2 inference on the M1 Max is bounded and
Bacformer is a one-shot transformer pass over a few thousand tokens. For analysing a handful of MAGs from Dan's own work
or a paper, runtime is seconds to minutes.

**KnowledgeBase implications:** Bacformer outputs are natural KG entities. Predicted operons become contiguous-gene
edges; PPIs become protein-protein edges weighted by contextual cosine similarity; phenotype inferences become
genome→trait edges with attribution-based gene→phenotype evidence. The pre-annotated 1.3M-MAG phenotype resource is
itself a candidate KnowledgeBase ingest.

**Architectural transfer — genome-as-sequence-of-proteins as a pattern:** Bacformer's core move is to take an existing
per-element foundation model (ESM-2 over proteins) and add a _second-order_ transformer that contextualises each element
by its position in a larger ordered structure. The same recipe could apply to codebase-as-ordered-sequence-of-functions,
notebook-as-ordered-sequence-of-cells, paper-corpus-as-ordered-sequence-of-claims. Worth keeping as a Linus design
template for any domain where individual elements have foundation-model embeddings but their structural ordering carries
meaning.

**Phase 1 (Recon & Baselines):** Load Bacformer from HuggingFace, run zero-shot operon prediction on a public _E. coli_
genome, compare to Operon DB — a one-day Worker task that produces a measured datapoint for "Apple Silicon viability of
mid-size bio foundation models" before any deeper Phase 7 commitment.

## What's NOT applicable

The training pipeline is firmly HPC: 16 Intel Ponte Vecchio GPUs for 30 days of embedding, plus 4 A100s for 2 weeks of
pretraining. Reproducing pretraining on the M1 Max is not on any Linus roadmap; the realistic path is to _use the
released checkpoints_ and fine-tune at most. The MAG corpus itself (5.6 TB of ESM-2 protein embeddings) is also not
Linus-storage-tractable as a whole — Dan would pull subsets relevant to a specific question.

The biological scope is bacterial only. Archaea, viruses (phage in particular — connects to Wave-3 generative biology),
and eukaryotes are out of training distribution; performance there is not measured. For Dan's microbial-ecology work
this is mostly a feature, but if he wants a Bacformer-equivalent for phage genomics that's a separate model (Evo and
successors are closer for that domain).

The generative claims, while exciting, are at the level of _protein-family sequences_, not synthesised amino acid
chains. The contrastive-loss extension that lets specific protein sequences be retrieved is a partial fix, but the
"synthesise a real bacterial genome" promise is still gated on downstream wet-lab synthesis and DNA writing
infrastructure that Bacformer does not provide. For Linus this means: useful for in-silico hypothesis generation, not
for end-to-end "Linus designed this strain."

The dual-use safety surface is non-trivial — the authors include an explicit safety-and-ethics section noting that
whole-genome generative models could lower the bar for designing pathogens, and that regulatory frameworks (CDC/NIH
Recombinant Nucleic Acids guidelines, BMBL, Federal Select Agent Program, EU Directive 2001/18/EC, Cartagena Protocol)
currently target physical organisms rather than computational tools. Any Phase 7 Linus skill exposing the _generative_
head needs to inherit a SAFETY.md autonomy-tier consideration; the analytical heads (operon, essentiality, phenotype,
function) carry far less risk and are the natural first integration target.

Bacformer is not a chat or coding model; nothing about its capabilities improves Linus's Maestro/Worker orchestration
directly, and it would not help with Phase 2 MVP work. It earns its keep specifically when Dan's domain task is the
active one.

## Connections

**Group A wave — explicit contrasts.** REMME/REBEAN ([2026.02.09.704801v1.md](2026.02.09.704801v1.md)) operates on raw
nucleotide reads at the per-read level for individual function annotation; Bacformer is the **whole-genome contextual**
counterpart, working at the protein-token level over ordered genomes. Complementary, not redundant: REMME/REBEAN handles
short-read metagenomic snippets where assembly fails or function-per-read is what's needed; Bacformer handles assembled
MAGs or complete genomes where genome-wide context is the substrate. A combined workflow — REMME/REBEAN for read-level
annotation of unassembled fractions, Bacformer for genome-level context of MAGs — is plausibly stronger than either
alone.

LucaOne is sequence-only at the single-protein level; Bacformer adds genome-level context. ProteinReasoner
([2025.07.21.665832v2.md](2025.07.21.665832v2.md)) injects evolutionary-profile reasoning into single-protein
generation; Bacformer reasons across proteins within a genome. Evo and Evo 2 are nucleotide-level whole-genome LMs —
Bacformer beats fine-tuned Evo on bacterial gene essentiality, suggesting the protein-token abstraction is the better
inductive bias for _bacterial_ genomes (Evo's nucleotide vocabulary spends capacity on coding-region detection that
Bacformer takes for granted via ESM-2).

**Foundation-model architectural pattern.** Bacformer is a clean instance of "foundation model over foundation-model
embeddings" — frozen ESM-2 below, trained transformer above. The same hierarchical pattern Linus's orchestration layer
imposes when composing Worker outputs into multi-step pipelines, and cheaper than end-to-end retraining. Worth tracking
as a recurring design move.

**KnowledgeBase / RAG.** Phenotype-attribution outputs (gene → trait importance scores) are exactly the weighted edges a
RAG-augmented LLM could query when Dan asks "what genes drive sporulation in this MAG?" Bacformer-as-tool could plug
into KnowledgeBase as both annotator (writes new edges) and retriever (answers gene-function queries against its
1.3M-MAG annotation cache).

   numerical tolerance.

   pattern, or premature?
