# RiNALMo: general-purpose RNA language models can generalize well on structure prediction tasks

## TL;DR

RiNALMo is a 650M-parameter BERT-style encoder-only Transformer pre-trained with masked language modeling (MLM) on **36M
curated non-coding RNA (ncRNA) sequences** from RNAcentral, Rfam, nt, and Ensembl. It uses the modern Transformer recipe
— RoPE, SwiGLU, FlashAttention-2 — and is the largest RNA LM with publicly released weights. Fine-tuned on six
downstream tasks (intra- and inter-family secondary structure, multi-species splice-site, ncRNA family classification,
MRL, translation efficiency, expression level), it is SOTA or near-SOTA across the board. The headline result is
**inter-family generalization for secondary structure prediction**: prior DL methods (UFold, MXfold2, SPOT-RNA, RNA-FM)
collapse on held-out families and lose to thermodynamic baselines (RNAstructure, CONTRAfold); RiNALMo beats both DL
competitors and thermodynamic baselines on eight of nine ArchiveII families — the first learned RNA model to do this
convincingly. Weights and fine-tuning scripts are public (GitHub `lbcb-sci/RiNALMo`, weights on Zenodo).

## The problem (in plain language)

RNA is no longer just the messenger between DNA and protein — it is itself an emerging drug target, a regulator, a
structural element, and a catalyst. To do anything serious with it computationally, one wants to predict structure
(which bases pair with which) and function (what the molecule does in the cell) from sequence. Both are hard, and both
are hindered by data scarcity at the structural level: there are vastly fewer RNA structures in the PDB than protein
structures, and good multiple sequence alignments (MSAs) — the workhorse evolutionary signal that AlphaFold2 leans on —
are much harder to build for RNA. The natural workaround, demonstrated repeatedly for proteins by ESM-1b/ESM-2/OmegaPLM,
is to train a large self-supervised language model on the much more abundant _unlabeled_ sequence data, then transfer
the learned representations to the data-scarce labeled tasks.

For RNA, two prior single-sequence foundation models exist: RNA-FM (100M parameters, 23.7M ncRNAs, public) and Uni-RNA
(25M–400M, 1B sequences, _not_ public, claims plateau above 400M). Both showed the protein-LM playbook transfers, but
neither produced a model that _generalizes_ to RNA families unseen during fine-tuning — the central practical limitation
of learned RNA structure predictors. A model that only handles familiar families is not much use for the long tail of
newly discovered ncRNAs, which is where computational prediction matters most.

Penić et al.'s bet is that Uni-RNA's reported plateau is an architecture/dataset artifact, not a fundamental limit, and
that pushing past 400M with modern primitives plus a more diverse training set will produce representations that
generalize across families.

## What they propose

RiNALMo is an encoder-only Transformer with **33 blocks**, a **1280-dim embedding**, **20 attention heads**, and
**3413-dim FFN hidden** (with SwiGLU gating), totaling **650M parameters**. The architectural deltas from RNA-FM are the
parts a 2024-era practitioner would write down without thinking: rotary positional embeddings (RoPE) instead of learned
absolute position embeddings, SwiGLU instead of GELU, and FlashAttention-2 for IO-aware exact attention. The authors run
a small ablation (Supplementary S14, S15) confirming both RoPE and SwiGLU each contribute measurable gains over the
RNA-FM-style choices. Tokenization is single-nucleotide (no BPE), with the IUPAC ambiguity codes plus inosine and the
standard MLM specials (`[CLS]`, `[EOS]`, `[PAD]`, `[MASK]`); `U` is normalized to `T`. Sequences are clipped to 1024
tokens during pre-training (random crop if longer).

The data pipeline is one of the more careful contributions. They pull ncRNA from RNAcentral, Rfam, nt, and Ensembl, drop
sequences shorter than 16 or longer than 8192, deduplicate with `seqkit rmdup`, then **cluster with
`mmseqs easy-linclust` at 70% identity / 80% coverage**, yielding 36M unique sequences in 17M clusters. The
training-loop trick: each mini-batch samples one sequence per cluster, so each epoch sees 17M _diverse_ samples rather
than 36M biased ones. The authors attribute the inter-family generalization gain partly to this diversity sampling — a
clean example of "data curation as architecture."

Pre-training is standard MLM (15%/80/10/10), trained on **7× A100 80GB for 2 weeks** at batch 192/GPU, cosine LR with
linear warm-up (peak 5e-5).

Each downstream task gets a small head on the (gradually unfrozen) encoder. Secondary structure: outer-concatenated
per-pair tensor → 2-block ResNet → pairing logit matrix → greedy decoder (no pseudoknot, no sharp hairpin, canonical
pairings only). Splice-site and ncRNA family classification: `[CLS]` → 2-layer MLP. MRL/TE/EL regression: 5'UTR token
embeddings → 1D ResNet. Fine-tuning uses **gradual unfreezing** (head first, then +3 encoder layers every 3 epochs).

## Key results

On **intra-family secondary structure** (bpRNA-derived TS0, train and test from same families): RiNALMo beats SPOT-RNA,
UFold, MXfold2, and RNA-FM on precision, recall, and F1.

On **structure-dissimilar held-out** (TrainSetA → TestSetB, the Rivas et al. benchmark): RiNALMo beats RNAstructure,
CONTRAfold, MXfold2, and RNA-FM on F1.

On **inter-family generalization** (ArchiveII, 9 families, leave-one-family-out): the headline. RNA-FM, UFold, and
MXfold2 are systematically worse than the thermodynamic baselines (RNAstructure, CONTRAfold) — a known and damning
failure mode for learned RNA structure predictors. RiNALMo wins on **8 of 9 families** by large margins. The single
failure is **telomerase RNA**, which is ~25% longer than the next-longest family and clusters confusingly close to SRP
RNA in the embedding space. RiNALMo also matches or exceeds homology-based tools (CentroidHomfold, locARNA,
CentroidAlifold) on every family except telomerase.

On **multi-species splice-site prediction** (donor + acceptor, 4 held-out species): RiNALMo's average F1 (97.70 Fish,
96.11 Fly, 96.25 Plant, 95.63 Worm) beats SpliceBERT — a model pre-trained _exclusively_ on pre-mRNA — and beats
Spliceator, Uni-RNA, RNA-FM. A model never trained on a single mRNA outperforms one specifically built for mRNA.

On **ncRNA family classification** (88 Rfam families, ±200% boundary noise): RiNALMo averages 0.989/0.937 vs.
0.985/0.915 for Uni-RNA and 0.840–0.865 for k-mer CNN baselines.

On **MRL / TE / EL** (5'UTR translation tasks): RiNALMo wins R² on Random7600/Human7600 (0.92/0.86) and wins Spearman on
TE/EL across Muscle, PC3, HEK cell lines — despite never seeing mRNA or UTR data during pre-training. UTR-LM, the
purpose-built model, is second.

A perplexity ablation across 33M / 150M / 650M shows monotonic improvement with scale on pre-training and on every
downstream task — Uni-RNA's reported plateau at 400M does not hold here.

## What's reusable in Linus

**Phase 7 (Skills & Autonomy), direct:** RiNALMo is a clean candidate for a Linus "RNA structure prediction" skill.
Weights on Zenodo (`zenodo.org/records/15043668`), repo at `lbcb-sci/RiNALMo`, auto-download script included. At 650M
parameters the model fits comfortably in M1 Max unified memory (a few GB at fp16, sub-GB at int4). Inference at typical
ncRNA lengths (≤1024 tokens) is firmly tractable on Metal/MLX. The downstream heads are small and fine-tuning scripts
are public, so a Linus "predict secondary structure" tool is a packaging job, not a research project.

**Phase 6 (Fine-Tuning), edge-of-comfort:** 650M is at the upper edge of LoRA fine-tuning on 32 GB unified memory. ESM-2
650M LoRA on Apple Silicon has been done by the community; the recipe should transfer. This is the obvious "Dan wants a
domain-specialized RNA Worker" path: LoRA-tune RiNALMo-650M on a subset of interest (riboswitches, structure-probed
RNAs, etc.) and serve locally. The paper's _full_ unfrozen fine-tuning is **not** comfortable on 32 GB — LoRA is the
right tactic.

**Phase 1 (Recon & Baselines), direct:** RiNALMo on its published downstream tasks (TS0, ArchiveII held-out family, GS_1
splice-site) gives a reproducible reference for "does the local Apple Silicon stack produce the same numbers as the
paper's A100 runs?" — a useful sanity check for MLX/Metal inference correctness on a non-trivial scientific model.

**Architecture transfer, Phase 6+:** **RoPE + SwiGLU + FlashAttention-2 + single-nucleotide tokenization +
diversity-clustered batching** is the modern encoder Transformer recipe any future Linus-trained domain LM will likely
use. The 17M-cluster-once-per-epoch sampling is particularly cheap to copy — it's just data-loading code — and produced
measurable generalization gains here.

**Generalization framing, conceptual:** A _general-purpose_ RNA LM beating _specialists_ on their own turf (RiNALMo >
SpliceBERT on splicing; > UTR-LM on translation, despite never seeing pre-mRNA or UTR data) is the same lesson the
protein-LM community learned with ESM-2: a sufficiently large self-supervised model on a diverse corpus dominates narrow
specialists. This argues for Linus's tool registry being organized around _broad_ foundation models (one RNA, one
protein, one DNA, one small-molecule) rather than a sprawl of task-specific predictors.

## What's NOT applicable

The paper offers nothing on the inference side beyond standard PyTorch — no Apple Silicon, no MLX, no quantization.
Whoever packages RiNALMo for Linus handles the conversion (PyTorch+MPS, MLX, or Core ML). Single-nucleotide tokenization
plus 1024-token context is small, so this should be straightforward, but it is real work the paper doesn't do.

7×A100-for-2-weeks pre-training is not reproducible on Linus hardware. The right posture is _consume the released
weights_, not retrain.

The paper is silent on tertiary structure prediction. They speculate RiNALMo embeddings could replace MSAs in an RNA
AlphaFold-style pipeline (analogous to ESMFold), but they don't build it, and tertiary structure remains the hard RNA
problem. Linus shouldn't advertise tertiary capability based on RiNALMo alone.

The model is ncRNA-only — no mRNA coding sequences in pre-training. Transfer to mRNA tasks is impressive as a transfer
result, but for mRNA-specific work (codon optimization, full-CDS analysis) a model trained on the appropriate data is
the right tool.

Telomerase RNA is the documented failure mode. A Linus skill built on RiNALMo should surface this when telomerase-class
inputs are detected.

No inference latency, memory footprint, or batched-throughput numbers are reported. Those need to be measured locally
before deployment decisions.

## Connections

This is one of the **Group A** domain-foundation-model papers alongside Evo 2, LucaOne, AlphaGenome, Bacformer,
ProteinReasoner, METL, and REMME/REBEAN. RiNALMo is the _narrow specialist_ end: RNA-only, ncRNA-pre-trained, BERT-style
encoder. **LucaOne** is the _generalist_ counterpart — joint DNA/RNA/protein pre-training, on the bet that cross-modal
context beats single-modality scaling. The comparison is not yet settled; RiNALMo's strong inter-family generalization
and beating of mRNA specialists despite ncRNA-only training is one data point in favor of "modality-focused with diverse
data within modality."

The architectural recipe (RoPE + SwiGLU + FlashAttention-2) is the same one the BitNet line (`2310.11453v1`,
`2402.17764v1`, `2411.04965v1`, `2504.18415v2`) and most modern decoder LLMs converge on. RiNALMo is a useful proof
point that this recipe dominates encoder-only scientific FMs too. The diversity-clustered batching parallels the FineWeb
(`2406.17557v2`) finding that _what_ you train on matters as much as _how much_ — careful curation buys large downstream
gains for free.

The specialist-vs-generalist FM tension parallels the harness/orchestration distinction in `GLOSSARY.md`: the generalist
substrate (LucaOne, ESM-2) tends to outlive the constellation of narrow specialists (RNA-MSM, SpliceBERT, UTR-LM,
potentially RiNALMo itself) as it scales.

The KnowledgeBase pillar can intersect directly: RiNALMo embeddings could be co-indexed alongside paper embeddings in
Qdrant, enabling "find papers about RNAs structurally similar to this sequence" — a Phase 3 idea that becomes possible
the moment the model is loadable.
