# Self-Driving Labs Power Up

## TL;DR
Nature feature on the rise of autonomous, AI-powered robotic laboratories that automate scientific experimentation end-to-end. Covers roboticists and chemists building self-driving labs using LLM-guided hypothesis generation, Bayesian optimization, and autonomous experimentation. Examples include Eve (University of Manchester), Genesis (Ginkgo Bioworks), and emerging ventures (Periodic Labs, LabGenAI, Lila Sciences, Fedus/OpenAI). Paradigm shift: from human-directed experiments to AI directing thousands of well-designed experiments per day.

## The problem (in plain language)
Scientific discovery is bottlenecked by human experimental cycles: labs run handful of experiments per week due to human labor costs, slow manual hypothesis generation, and design inefficiencies. Autonomous robotics exists but lacks the AI guidance to efficiently explore hypothesis space. LLMs now enable the missing layer: high-level scientific reasoning to direct robotic experimentation.

## What they propose
Integrated systems combining:

1. **LLM-guided hypothesis generation**: Models like Claude (Anthropic) and GPT (OpenAI) generate experimental hypotheses and design optimized compounds/conditions
2. **Robotic automation**: Hardware (Eve, Genesis, MicroCycle) executes experiments under robotic control with minimal human intervention
3. **Bayesian optimization**: Iterative refinement of experimental design based on results, avoiding human-biased parameter selection
4. **Closed-loop learning**: Results feed back to LLM for next hypothesis, enabling rapid iteration (1000+ experiments/day feasible)

Real examples: Eve's robot scientist autonomously tested malaria drug compounds; Genesis discovered terpene-synthesis pathways; Periodic Labs' GPT-guided chemistry, Fedus/OpenAI's materials discovery.

## Key results
- **Eve (Ross King, University of Manchester)**: Autonomously discovered novel antimicrobial compounds in dormant Plasmodium parasites; identified tricosan target; ran 1,600+ chemical screens in parallel
- **Genesis (Ginkgo Bioworks/CMU)**: Autonomous lab with "self-driving" design; 30% cost reduction in protein production assays (Hyundai project); yield improvement via closed-loop optimization
- **Throughput**: Autonomous labs can perform thousands of well-designed experiments daily vs. manual labs' dozen per week
- **Cost-effectiveness**: Periodic Labs estimates 50-100x cost reduction in drug screening vs. traditional HTS; Lila Sciences targeting cost parity with computational chemistry while preserving evidence trail
