# BioReason-Pro: Advancing Protein Function Prediction with Multimodal Biological Reasoning

## TL;DR

BioReason-Pro is a multimodal reasoning LLM (Qwen3-4B backbone) that fuses ESM3 residue-level protein embeddings, a Gene
Ontology graph encoder, and biological context (organism, InterPro domains, STRING PPIs, localization) with **GO term
hypotheses from a companion autoregressive transformer called GO-GPT**, then writes a structured chain-of-thought trace
producing functional annotations and predicted interaction partners. Trained via SFT on ~130K GPT-5-generated reasoning
traces over 3,135 organisms, then refined with GSPO reinforcement learning using weighted Fmax as reward. Headlines:
GO-GPT alone hits weighted Fmax = 0.65 single-run / 0.70 best-of-10, beating CAFA-5-class baselines; BioReason-Pro
reaches 73.6% (unweighted) Fmax; an LLM-judge gives functional summaries 8.03/10; **27 human protein experts on 162
proteins prefer-or-tie the SFT model's annotations against UniProt ground truth in 79% of cases (RL: 73%)**. Per-residue
attention from the partner-prediction token localizes to the exact contact residues resolved in cryo-EM structures
(eEFSec/SBP2 at the SECIS interface in PDB 7ZJW; CFAP61's degenerate Rossmann active site at the dynein contact in PDB
8J07). Weights, code, dataset, web interface, and pre-computed annotations for >240K proteins (including the Human
Protein Atlas) are released at bioreason.net and Hugging Face.

## The problem (in plain language)

UniProt has over 250 million protein sequences and fewer than 0.1% have experimental functional annotations.
Computational annotation has historically been one of two things: BLAST/DIAMOND homology transfer (which collapses for
proteins far from training homologs), or per-GO-term classifiers (NetGO, InterLabelGO+, ProtBoost, DPFunc) that treat
each of ~40K Gene Ontology terms as an independent label. Neither resembles what an expert biologist actually does,
which is reason: combine sequence, structural fold, domain architecture, evolutionary context, organism, and known
interaction networks into a coherent functional hypothesis with an explicit "and therefore" between the evidence and the
conclusion.

Two trends have ripened separately. Protein foundation models (ESM3, ProtT5) encode rich sequence-structure-function
geometry but only implicitly. And general LLMs equipped with explicit chain-of-thought dramatically outperform
direct-answer baselines on multi-step problems. A small body of free-text protein annotation work (Prot2Text, ProtT3,
STELLA, ProtChatGPT) has aligned protein embeddings with LLMs to produce natural-language summaries, but those models
_describe_, they do not _reason_ — they don't justify why a given function is plausible from the available evidence.
This paper is the first attempt to put the two together for proteins.

## What they propose

**Three-stage pipeline.** First, ESM3 produces residue-level embeddings from sequence. Second, **GO-GPT**, an
autoregressive transformer the authors built, reads those embeddings plus organism plus the GO aspect being predicted,
and emits a sequence of GO terms — treating the ontology hierarchy as a generation task rather than as ~40K independent
classification heads. The mask is "prefix-causal": protein residues attend bidirectionally; GO tokens cross-attend to
all residues and causally attend to preceding GO tokens. ESM2 is used inside GO-GPT for batched-embedding efficiency.
Third, the **BioReason-Pro reasoning LLM** (Qwen3-4B) consumes ESM3 embeddings, a separate GO-graph encoder, GO-GPT's
term hypotheses, InterPro domains, STRING interactions, localization, organism, and structure cross-references, and
emits a structured CoT: domain analysis → molecular function → cellular component → biological process → mechanistic
explanation → predicted binding partners → final annotation block.

**Training recipe** is the [BioReason (2505.23579v2)](2505.23579v2.md) recipe ported to proteins. GPT-5 is given each
training protein's metadata bundle and prompted to write a structured reasoning trace ending with the answer; ~130K
traces, ~140M tokens, averaging 1,100+ tokens each, with manual spot-checks. Then SFT next-token prediction on those
traces. Then GSPO reinforcement learning (Group Sequence Policy Optimization, 2025) with weighted Fmax between predicted
and ground-truth GO terms as reward. Temporal split follows CAFA — train through Nov 2022, test on 8,630 proteins that
gained new experimental annotations between March 2023 and February 2024.

**Methodological note on the 79% headline.** Not an automated metric. 27 molecular biologists, blinded to model
identity, compared each model's annotation against UniProt ground truth on a five-point preference scale (significantly
falls short → significantly exceeds). The 79%/73% numbers are the **tie-or-exceed** rate — strict "exceeds" is smaller.
Evaluators also scored each prediction 1–10 on seven axes. Crucially, experts who preferred SFT did so on Mechanistic
Depth; experts who preferred RL did so on Hallucination avoidance. The model trades off mechanism-depth against factual
reliability, and curated UniProt entries — deliberately conservative and terse — sit on a different point of that
trade-off. The honest reading is not "the model is better than UniProt"; it's "domain experts find these annotations
at-least-as-useful as UniProt's, in part because they include explanatory mechanism UniProt doesn't." Style and depth
are doing real work alongside accuracy.

## Key results

**GO-GPT against prior SOTA.** Weighted Fmax 0.65 single-run greedy, 0.67 with 10-sample term-frequency probabilities,
0.70 best-of-10 oracle. InterLabelGO+ (top accessible CAFA-5 method) sits at 0.63. Unweighted: GO-GPT 0.76 vs
InterLabelGO+ 0.70. Significant for BP and CC; MF under greedy is borderline (p = 0.073). Autoregressive sampling gives
free per-term confidence estimates without separate calibration.

**BioReason-Pro on GO prediction.** SFT 0.64 weighted Fmax greedy / 0.67 best-of-10. RL 0.66 / 0.67 — RL concentrates
probability mass on correct predictions. Best unweighted Fmax 0.74 (the 73.6% in the abstract is this unweighted
best-of-10 number, not the weighted figure).

**LLM-judge (GPT-5.1, 1–10 across MF/BP/CC/Specificity/Reliability).** RL averages 8.03; SFT 7.65; Prot2Text-v2
(strongest free-text baseline) 4.15. RL improves Reliability from 6.92 to 7.56 (less hallucination); SFT edges RL on
Specificity (8.74 vs 8.59).

**Generalization across sequence similarity.** BLAST falls off sharply with identity to training (Spearman ρ = 0.43 on
judge score). BioReason-Pro shows roughly half that dependence (RL ρ = 0.27, SFT ρ = 0.21), and its advantage over BLAST
grows as similarity drops. Even at high-similarity bins where close homologs are available, BioReason-Pro still beats
BLAST — the reasoning is doing work beyond what alignment recovers.

**Human expert evaluation.** 27 experts, 162 proteins, blinded. SFT tie-or-exceed UniProt: 79%; RL: 73% (difference not
significant; McNemar p = 0.087). Expert overall scores: SFT 8.0/10, RL 7.4/10. Reasoning, Mechanistic Depth, and
Hallucination scores correlate strongly within each model — well-reasoned predictions also avoid hallucinations and
provide deeper mechanism. Performance is stable across BLAST identity bins, organisms, protein lengths, and InterPro
domain counts.

**Cryo-EM-validated attention** is the interpretability headline. (1) **eEFSec** (P57772, selenocysteine-specific
elongation factor): with no PPI context, BioReason-Pro SFT predicts SBP2 as the obligate functional partner — confirmed
in the 2.8 Å cryo-EM selenosome structure (PDB 7ZJW). Per-residue attention from the token preceding the partner name
localizes sharply to the C-terminal RIFT domain (p = 1.2 × 10⁻⁵³ vs tr-GTPase), and within RIFT to the exact SECIS-RNA
contact residues (Mann–Whitney p = 2.3 × 10⁻⁵). GPT-5.2 Thinking High misidentifies eEFSec as a septin-family GTPase.
(2) **CFAP61** (Q8NHU2): an axonemal scaffold whose Rossmann-like FAD/NAD(P)-binding fold cues "oxidoreductase" in
standard pipelines, but whose 12 classical catalytic positions are all degenerate. BioReason-Pro reads the architectural
context (flanking axonemal-targeting and dimerization domains, cilia-specific family) and overrides the superfamily
annotation, predicting non-enzymatic scaffolding. Attention concentrates on three repurposed positions (C734, V737,
V739) — the exact residues forming direct contacts with dynein heavy chain 1 in the cryo-EM axoneme structure (PDB
8J07).

**Stress ablation.** On synthetic AI-designed Acr proteins (no homologs, no InterPro hits), SFT fabricates InterPro
entries on some short peptides; RL never does. Predictions for the same novel sequence diverge across organism labels
(E. coli K12 vs O157:H7 vs human) — organism prior dominates when intrinsic evidence is thin. Below ~50 amino acids,
performance degrades systematically.

## What's reusable in Linus

**Phase 7 protein-function annotation skill (direct).** Strongest candidate for the Linus protein-function tool. Weights
for GO-GPT, BioReason-Pro SFT, and BioReason-Pro RL are on Hugging Face (bioreason.net/gogpt, /sft, /rl); code at
bioreason.net/code; pre-computed annotations for >240K proteins at bioreason.net/atlas. The Qwen3-4B backbone plausibly
runs on the M1 Max in 4–8 bit quantization; ESM3 is the only "large" companion and embeddings cache per protein; GO-GPT
is small. Inference cost is the worry (three sequential models) but modularity helps. A Phase 1 spike running
BioReason-Pro on a handful of Dan's proteins of interest is a high-information cycle.

**The "typed-prediction companion" pattern (transferable).** GO-GPT is the most generalizable piece. Train an
autoregressive transformer over a _typed vocabulary_ (GO terms as tokens), with cross-attention to a representation
channel and causal attention over the type-DAG; you get per-token confidence-by-sampling for free, capture
hierarchical/cross-aspect dependencies that per-class classifiers miss, and produce discrete-token output that
downstream reasoners can treat as evidence. Generalizes to KEGG (Pathway-GPT), Reactome, MeSH, ChEBI, EC numbers. Worth
flagging in `docs/synthesis/` as a recurring Group-C pattern.

**Worker-output schema (architectural).** BioReason-Pro's trace structure — observation → typed predictions → mechanism
→ hypotheses → final answer block — is a template for how Linus Worker outputs should be shaped on evidence-reasoning
tasks: not just an answer, but the chain that produced it, with typed predictions separable from prose. Audit log
benefits twice: structured fields queryable, prose LLM-judge-auditable for hallucination as in Section 2.3.

**KnowledgeBase content (direct).** Pre-computed BioReason-Pro annotations for 240K proteins are an immediate KB
ingestion target — they include explanation, not just classification. For any protein Dan looks up, the KB returns the
structured CoT alongside the GO terms — the _why_ not just the _what_. Materially higher-quality KG content than
UniProt's bare term lists.

**Memory-synthesis citation.** Add to `docs/synthesis/memory-pillar.md`: BioReason-Pro is another empirical
instantiation of explicit CoT producing expert-preferred outputs in a scientific domain, and uniquely a case where
cryo-EM attention interpretability provides external validation that the reasoning chain grounds in the right evidence —
not just plausible prose.

**Evaluation-harness pattern (for benchmarks/dan_tasks/).** The four-tier eval — automated Fmax, LLM-judge, human
pairwise, structural grounding — is a template for evaluating Linus outputs in domains where ground truth is itself
contested.

## What's NOT applicable

Training compute is large — 130K GPT-5 traces (~140M tokens) plus SFT plus GSPO RL on a 4B base model is not a Phase 1
reproduction. Linus uses BioReason-Pro as a _tool_, not something it trains. Re-running the recipe for a different
ontology (KEGG, Reactome) is Phase 6+ work requiring a synthetic-trace generator (GPT-5 or successor), GPU time, and a
curated evidence pipeline.

The cryo-EM attention validation is a _result_, not a method Linus applies broadly. It worked because eEFSec and CFAP61
happen to have high-resolution complex structures with annotated contact residues — most proteins don't. Per-residue
attention as an interpretability signal is general; cryo-EM ground truth for it is rare.

The 79%-tie-or-exceed-UniProt result is striking but should not be paraphrased as "the model is better than UniProt." It
mixes accuracy, mechanistic depth, and readability against UniProt's deliberately conservative style. Linus quoting it
should preserve that nuance — overclaiming would itself be the kind of thing penalized under "Hallucination" in the
eval.

Strain-sensitive predictions on synthetic Acr proteins (same sequence → different annotation in K12 vs O157:H7 vs human)
are a real failure mode — organism label dominates when intrinsic evidence is thin. For metagenomic or designed
proteins, the model is less reliable than its main-benchmark numbers suggest. Linus should surface this in the UI, not
hide it.

Qwen3-4B is current as of this paper but model lineage moves fast. By Phase 7, the question is whether the recipe ports
cleanly to whatever 4–8B base model fits Apple Silicon then. The architecture is portable; released weights may not be
the best instantiation for Linus's runtime.

## Connections

**Protein evolution of [BioReason (2505.23579v2)](2505.23579v2.md)** — same lead authors (Fallahpour et al.,
Wang/Goodarzi labs, Arc Institute/UHN), same recipe (foundation-model embeddings + synthetic CoT traces from a frontier
LLM + SFT + RL), different modality (DNA-LM → ESM3 protein-LM). BioReason-Pro's main architectural delta is the GO-GPT
typed-prediction companion as an extra evidence channel. Reading the pair is the cleanest way to see what generalizes
across the recipe.

**Closest neighbor to [ProteinReasoner (2025.07.21.665832v2)](2025.07.21.665832v2.md).** Both add explicit reasoning to
protein FMs, with different machinery: ProteinReasoner uses evolutionary profiles as a typed CoT step inside the
reasoning chain; BioReason-Pro uses the autoregressive GO-GPT companion plus free-text CoT from a separate reasoning
LLM. ProteinReasoner is "reasoning is a single-model behavior trained by PPO"; BioReason-Pro is "reasoning is a pipeline
with a typed-prediction stage and a free-text stage." Both are Phase 7 candidates; they're not mutually exclusive.

**Paradigm contrast to [ProtHGT (2025.04.19.649272v1)](2025.04.19.649272v1.md)** for the same task (GO prediction).
ProtHGT runs heterogeneous graph transformers over a protein–GO–domain–pathway KG; BioReason-Pro runs CoT over
multimodal embeddings. ProtHGT is fast, KG-native, returns ranked GO terms; BioReason-Pro is slow, narrative-native,
returns explanations. They could compose: ProtHGT's KG-derived GO ranking as another evidence channel into
BioReason-Pro's reasoning context, alongside or instead of GO-GPT.

**Empirical fuel for the memory-pillar CoT thread** — paper-notes/2305.15408v5.md (Saparov & He), 2310.07923v5.md (Wei
survey), 2205.11916v4.md (Kojima zero-shot CoT), 2412.06769v3.md (Coconut latent reasoning). BioReason-Pro is the
Group-C scientific-domain instantiation, and one of the few cases where external ground truth (cryo-EM structures)
validates that the reasoning chain is _learning the right thing_ rather than merely producing plausible prose.

## Open questions for Dan

1. **Phase 7 deployment path:** Weights, code, and a 240K-protein pre-computed atlas are all released. Should the first
   concrete Phase 7 protein-function tool be (a) a thin wrapper over the bioreason.net inference API, (b) a local
   deployment of GO-GPT + Qwen3-4B + ESM3 on the M1 Max, or (c) ingestion of the 240K-protein atlas into the
   KnowledgeBase with on-demand inference layered later? (c) is the cheapest first move and might be the right Phase 1
   spike.

2. **Skill selection criteria:** Phase 7 will choose between BioReason-Pro, ProtHGT, ProteinReasoner, possibly Horizyn-1
   — or compose them. What axes? Latency, narrative quality, GO-Fmax, low-similarity robustness, ease of local
   deployment, agreement with experimental controls? The paper's eval stack (automated / LLM-judge / human pairwise /
   structural grounding) is a picking template; we should build a scaled-down version for Linus.

3. **Worker-output structure:** Should _all_ Linus Worker outputs reasoning over evidence adopt a BioReason-Pro-style
   structured envelope — observation, typed predictions, mechanism, hypothesis, final answer — separable from prose so
   the audit log has structured queryable fields and the human-facing answer has the chain attached? This would
   standardize "good Worker output" across protein annotation, code generation, and scientific Q&A.

4. **Pathway-GPT generalization:** The GO-GPT autoregressive-typed-prediction-companion trick should generalize directly
   to KEGG (Pathway-GPT), Reactome, MeSH, ChEBI, EC numbers. Is there a Linus-relevant typed vocabulary where this would
   be high-value to build? Training cost is the gating factor, not architecture.

5. **The 79% number, framed honestly:** Section 2.5 shows expert-SFT preference is driven by Mechanistic Depth and
   expert-RL preference by Hallucination avoidance — the models trade off, UniProt sits at a different point. For Linus,
   what matters more — annotation correctness or explanatory depth? The honest answer is "both, depending on task," but
   it has implications: a quality-check-UniProt task wants RL (low hallucination); a
   hypothesis-generation-for-novel-protein task wants SFT (richer mechanism). The tool's API should let the caller pick.

6. **Cryo-EM attention as a general interpretability practice:** Per-residue attention from the partner-prediction token
   concentrates on actual contact residues. Is per-token attention-localization an evaluation we should run on _any_
   Worker output where ground-truth localization exists (highlighted code spans for "this code is buggy because",
   citation spans for "the paper claims X")? The technique is general; cryo-EM is just one demonstration domain.
