# Multimodal foundation model predicts zero-shot functional perturbations and cell fate dynamics

## TL;DR

PertFormer is a 3-billion-parameter multimodal foundation model pretrained on **paired bulk epigenetics and single-cell
multiomics** (scATAC + scRNA across 1 million cells, 1.5 billion paired samples; plus 55 Gbp of ENCODE bulk data across
seven histone/accessibility marks and 34 cell lines/tissues). The distinctive move is to operate at the _gene_ unit —
each training sample is one gene's 300 kb regulatory window — expanding the effective dataset from "cells" to "gene ×
cells" and enabling **zero-shot in-silico perturbation**: simulate a CRISPRi knockdown or TF knock-in by editing
chromatin accessibility tracks at predicted regulatory loci, then read the predicted expression delta. Reported gains
over prior methods are 20.9%–480.2% across enhancer-gene linkage, TF perturbation, and cell-state-transition tasks
(zero-shot, no fine-tuning); two novel cancer targets (THAP2 in TNBC, ZIK1 in OV) are validated by siRNA knockdown and
survival analysis. Code: `alibaba/damo-pertformer`. Weights: `GenomicIntelligenceDamoAcademy/pertformer`. Trained on 32×
A100 in PyTorch bf16. **For Linus: the only paper in the corpus operating at the cell-state level, and the first
plausible Phase 7 candidate skill for cell-biology workflows.**

## The problem (in plain language)

A perturbation screen — CRISPRi, perturb-seq, TF knock-in — is the workhorse of modern functional genomics: knock down a
gene in a population of cells, sequence the result, learn what that gene was doing in that cell type. The trouble is
cost. Each cell-type × perturbation combination is a separate experiment, and the relevant biological space (every TF ×
every cell type × every disease microenvironment × multi-genic combinations) dwarfs any wet-lab budget. Most
perturbations a biologist actually wants to evaluate have never been run, and may never be.

The natural ML response is "predict the screen first, then run only the experiments that look most promising." Existing
approaches struggle in two complementary ways. DNA-sequence foundation models (Enformer, Borzoi, scBasset) can predict
expression from sequence and simulate base-pair mutations, but cannot natively represent an _epigenetic_ perturbation
(TF knockdown, regulator suppression, chromatin-mark change) — the input space is wrong. Single-cell transcriptomic
models (scGPT, Geneformer, scFoundation) capture cell states beautifully but were trained on transcriptomes alone; to
predict the effect of perturbing one gene out of ~20,000 they must detect a signal where <0.1% of input tokens have
changed, and most architectures don't. To get a usable perturbation signal, transcriptomic models need fine-tuning on
the very labeled data that is scarce.

PertFormer's bet is that joint pretraining on bulk epigenetics (mechanistic vocabulary: what a TF site looks like, what
an active enhancer looks like) and single-cell multiomics (cell-state vocabulary: which loci are accessible where)
breaks this deadlock. With both modalities unified, in-silico perturbation reduces to editing the chromatin track at the
right loci and re-predicting expression — no fine-tuning required.

## What they propose

PertFormer is a two-stage attention architecture. **PertFormer-Elementary** is the local module: it ingests a 32-kb
genomic window with up to seven epigenetic signal channels (DNA sequence + H3K4me3, H3K4me1, H3K27ac, H3K27me3, H3K9ac,
DNase, CTCF) tokenized at 128 bp resolution, and produces a per-1024-bp embedding summarizing the multiomic state of a
short regulatory element. Pretraining on bulkMultiomeCorpus-55b uses two self-supervised objectives: mask-reconstruction
on visible tracks, and "predict held-out modality from visible ones" to force cross-modality structure.

**PertFormer-Regulatory** is the long-range module, one gene at a time. It takes ~300 kb flanking the TSS, breaks it
into ~290 elements of 1024 bp, encodes each through PertFormer-Elementary (frozen), then runs a transformer with a
TSS-distance positional embedding (replacing standard sinusoidal positions) and attention pooling to integrate evidence
across the 290 elements. Output: predicted gene-expression for that (gene, cell). Pretrained on scMultiomeCorpus-1.5b (1
million 10x scMultiome cells; for each cell, every expressed gene yields one (gene, cell) sample → 1.5 B paired
samples). Parameters are shared across all genes; the model learns a single function (regulatory-context → expression)
that generalizes across the genome. Total: ~3 B parameters, trained on 32× A100 in PyTorch bf16 with distributed data
parallel.

What makes this a perturbation tool, not just a regulation predictor, is the **interpretation pipeline**. The _Attention
Score_ of a 1024-bp element on a gene comes straight from PertFormer-Regulatory's attention weights and ranks regulatory
importance for that (gene, cell). The _Attention Feature_ aggregates attention across all genes into a per-cell
signature capturing TF-level regulatory state. The in-silico perturbation protocol then: to simulate a TF KO, zero out
ATAC signal at loci enriched for that TF's motif; to simulate a TF KI, insert a high ATAC peak at TF-motif loci within
300 kb of the target gene's TSS; re-run PertFormer-Regulatory; read the delta. No fine-tuning, no labels.

## Key results

The paper sweeps four task families.

**Master regulators and GRNs.** The Attention Score on B cells of PBMCs reaches a mean Spearman 0.810 with ChIP-seq
signals of B-cell master regulators PAX5, EBF1, and POU2F2. On _unseen_ cells (CRC tumor cells not in
scMultiomeCorpus-1.5b), the score reaches Spearman 0.783 with SP1/FOSL1 ChIP-seq, beating pseudobulk scATAC's 0.582.
PertFormer recovers known cell-type-specific TFs across PBMC subsets (TCF7/LEF1/RUNX2 for T cells, IRF8 for DCs,
EOMES/TBX21 for NK cells, etc.) and recovers 67.4% of SCENIC+'s TF-to-gene relationships (with 46.7% overall agreement)
using only DNA + scATAC inputs.

**Enhancer-gene perturbation.** On the K562 CRISPRi-FlowFISH benchmark, PertFormer's Attention Score outperforms prior
best methods by an average of 60.3% in auPRC (range 11.2%–160.3%) for enhancer-gene linkage. On the VISTA mouse enhancer
set, mean improvement is 116.4% in auPRC. Simulated KO of validated enhancers and repressors recovers the experimental
sign of the expression change for 78.2% of up-regulated genes and 79.5% of down-regulated genes (macro-F1 0.759,
PCC_delta 0.525). Notably, 13 genomic elements that act as enhancer for one gene and repressor for another are correctly
resolved on both sides for 69.2% of cases.

**TF perturbation.** On ENCODE TF-KO and Norman perturb-seq TF-KI: macro-F1 0.789 (KO) and 0.754 (KI), PCC_delta 0.768
(KO) and 0.690 (KI). Other zero-shot models score macro-F1 < 0.5 and PCC_delta < 0.15. PertFormer in **zero-shot** still
beats fine-tuned competitors by 14.8%–29.9%.

**Cell-state transitions.** Simulated OSKM knock-in on fibroblasts, cardiomyocytes, and B cells reverses the
somatic→hESC DEG signature; cosine similarity to hESC rises from 0.215 to 0.747 while similarity to the somatic state
drops from 0.877 to 0.234. Macro-F1 for DEG direction is 0.795. Adding OSKM factors one at a time produces a monotonic
trajectory toward the stem-cell state. In mouse E18 NPCs, 33.78% of OSKM-perturbed cells reach mESC-like state.

**Wet-lab validation.** Zero-shot scans across 5 primary tumor types correctly recovered known
tumor-promoting/suppressing TFs and identified two novel candidates: THAP2 in TNBC and ZIK1 in OV. siRNA knockdown in
MDA-MB-231 (TNBC) and SKOV-3 (OV) achieved >65% KD efficiency; bulk RNA-seq confirmed the predicted shift toward
normal-epithelial cosine similarity; proliferation and migration (wound-healing, transwell) dropped significantly;
Kaplan-Meier on public data showed elevated survival probability for low-THAP2 TNBC and low-ZIK1 OV patients.

## What's reusable in Linus

**Phase 7 candidate skill — single-cell perturbation prediction.** The most concrete cell-biology skill in the corpus to
date. Code on GitHub, weights on Hugging Face. A "predict the effect of knocking down gene X in cell type Y" tool
registered to the Linus orchestration layer would be high-value for any project involving screen prioritization,
cell-fate engineering, or cancer-target nomination. The same machinery covers the inverse: given a cell-state delta
observed experimentally, identify which TF or enhancer perturbation is most consistent with it.

**Phase 7 candidate skill — zero-shot GRN inference.** The Attention Score / Attention Feature pipeline gives
cell-type-resolved master regulators and TF-target edges from DNA + scATAC alone, working on cell types absent from
pretraining — more useful than another SCENIC+ wrapper.

**KnowledgeBase content — perturbation triples.** (cell_type, perturbation, gene_response) is natural KG content.
PertFormer can populate this graph at scale; every (cell-type, TF-perturbation, target-gene) triple is a cheap inference
call. The resulting graph would let Dan ask "in which cellular contexts has this gene's regulatory function been
computationally predicted?" — a query currently impossible without bespoke pipelines per project.

**Generative-biology design loop.** PertFormer is the cellular analog of DISCO (in-silico screen → wet-lab validation).
The same design pattern Dan has thought about for molecules works one level up at cellular interventions. The THAP2/ZIK1
validation is a clean instance of the loop closing.

**Honest about scale.** 3 B parameters in bf16 ≈ 6 GB of weights — comfortably loadable on the M1 Max for inference. The
memory worry is not parameter count but the 300 kb × 290 element fan-out: a single forward pass for one gene runs
through PertFormer-Elementary 290 times. Whole-genome inference for one cell (~20,000 genes × 290 elements) is tractable
but not free; budget hours, not minutes. **Fine-tuning on M1 Max is out of scope** — 3 B is at the edge of LoRA
feasibility on 32 GB unified memory and the multimodal pipeline needs re-implementation outside CUDA. Inference-only for
now.

## What's NOT applicable / hype filter

**The pretraining recipe does not transfer.** 32× A100 + 1.5 B paired samples is not something Linus reproduces. The
reusable artifact is the released checkpoint, not the methodology.

**PyTorch + CUDA-native.** Code targets `torch.cuda.amp` bf16 on A100. Apple Silicon inference needs MPS porting,
numeric validation, and possibly MLX or Core ML conversion. Standard transformer blocks with attention pooling — nothing
exotic — so the port is mechanical but not free. Worth an experiment branch before committing.

**Human-only.** Pretraining is human ENCODE + 10x human scMultiome, with one mouse experiment (E18 brain) repurposed at
inference. Cross-species generalization to non-mammalian systems is unevaluated. For comparative genomics or microbial
work, this is the wrong tool.

**Requires scATAC for novel cell types.** Zero-shot to a new cell type requires that cell type's scATAC profile. If you
have only scRNA, you're back to the standard transcriptomic-FM regime where PertFormer offers less.

**No dosage, kinetics, or non-TF perturbations.** The simulator zeroes or peaks the ATAC track at TF-motif loci. It does
not model siRNA pharmacokinetics, drug binding, off-target effects, or non-TF regulatory perturbations (splice factors,
RBPs). Wet-lab will continue to surface effects the model does not predict.

**Zero-shot claim wants careful reading.** "Zero-shot" means "no fine-tuning on perturbation data" — not "model has
never seen the cell type." For included-cell evaluations the cell types _are_ in scMultiomeCorpus-1.5b. The unseen-cell
CRC results are the more honest zero-shot demonstrations and should be weighted accordingly when deciding whether to
trust the model on novel systems.

## Connections

**To Group A (sequence FMs).** Structurally complementary to Evo 2, LucaOne, and AlphaGenome. Group A produces
sequence/gene-level embeddings; PertFormer produces cell-state-conditioned regulatory embeddings. A natural composition:
take a LucaOne embedding for a gene, condition it on a PertFormer cell-state context, get a per-(gene, cell-type) vector
that respects both sequence semantics and regulatory environment. Not bridged in any released system, but obvious value
for "score this gene's likely importance in this specific tissue."

**To Group C neighbors.** ProtHGT (`2025.04.19.649272v1.md`) does protein-function prediction via heterogeneous graphs
over molecular entities; PertFormer does cellular-function prediction via attention over genomic regulatory context.
Both ask "what does this gene/protein _do_" at different scales — ProtHGT's answer is a GO term, PertFormer's is an
expression program in a cell type. BioReason / BioReason-Pro (`2505.23579v2.md`, `2026.03.19.712954v1.md`) layer
chain-of-thought reasoning over molecular FMs; PertFormer notably does _not_ add reasoning, betting purely on
representation quality and a single-pass simulator. A BioReason-style layer over PertFormer ("explain why knocking down
THAP2 should drop TNBC migration, citing regulatory edges") is a research direction the paper does not pursue. DIAMOND
DeepClust and Horizyn-1 sit at sequence/structure level, orthogonal to PertFormer's cell-state level.

**Group C wave-closing — themes for synthesis.** With this note Group C is complete (DIAMOND DeepClust, Horizyn-1,
ProtHGT, BioReason, BioReason-Pro, PertFormer). The synthesis should foreground: (1) all six attack _function
annotation_ at different abstraction levels — sequence, structure, protein graph, cell state — and a unified "function
FM" stack is a gap; (2) only BioReason adds explicit reasoning, the rest rely on representation; (3) PertFormer is the
modality outlier (cell rather than molecule) and may justify its own group label "cell-level FMs" if more accumulate
(Geneformer-2, sc-GPT successors, etc.).

**To Bacformer (`2025.07.20.665723v2.md`).** Bacformer encodes whole bacterial genomes; PertFormer encodes mammalian
cell regulatory state. A microbiome-host interaction project — "which gut microbe gene products perturb host enterocyte
cell state" — would naturally pair the two: Bacformer for the microbial side, PertFormer for the host-cell response.
Speculative but concrete enough to spec.

**To the orchestration layer.** A clean test case for the Phase 7 skill-registration pattern: non-trivial domain tool
with weights, code, interpretable I/O contract, obvious permission boundary (no autonomous wet-lab orders). If Linus can
register, route to, and audit this tool by Phase 7, the skill API design is probably right.
