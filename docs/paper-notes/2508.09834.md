# Speed Always Wins: A Survey on Efficient Architectures for Large Language Models

## TL;DR

Comprehensive taxonomy of efficient LLM architectures addressing transformer quadratic complexity bottlenecks: linear
sequence modeling, sparse attention, mixture-of-experts, hybrid designs, and diffusion LLMs. Directly applicable to
Linus phase 1 baseline evaluation and phase 6 fine-tuning on 32 GB Apple Silicon.

## The problem (in plain language)

Transformers scale poorly: self-attention is O(N²) in sequence length, making long-context inference expensive. Dense
FFN layers are compute-heavy. Standard attention requires full KV cache, consuming unbounded memory. Yet LLMs must
handle increasing context lengths (RAG, agent reasoning, multimodal inputs) efficiently.

## What they propose

Nine categories of optimization strategies, each with 20–100 methods surveyed:

1. **Linear Sequence Modeling** (Linear Attention, Linear RNN, State Space Models): O(N) via recurrent formulations
2. **Sparse Sequence Modeling** (Static/Dynamic/Training-free sparse attention): selective KV interactions
3. **Efficient Full Attention** (IO-aware, grouped, quantized): reduce memory access overhead without changing O(N²)
4. **Sparse Mixture-of-Experts**: conditional activation of expert subsets
5. **Hybrid Architectures**: mix linear + full attention layers strategically
6. **Diffusion LLMs**: non-autoregressive generation for speed
7. **Vision/Audio/Multimodality applications**: extend principles to other modalities

## Key results

- Taxonomy with ~300+ methods catalogued and referenced
- Compression trade-offs quantified: latency vs. throughput vs. accuracy
- Long-context patterns identified: RAG, agentic, reasoning, multimodal all favor sparse or linear attention
- Hybrid designs (50% linear, 50% full attention) achieve best practical throughput on real hardware

## What's reusable in Linus

**Phase 1 (Baselines) and Phase 6 (Fine-tuning) immediate relevance**:

- **Baseline selection**: Section 2–4 methods (linear/sparse attention) are candidates for MLX-based Worker models
  (Qwen2.5-7B variants, Mistral-7B). Linear RNNs and state-space models are Metal-friendly (no full KV materialization).
- **Long-context genomics**: Dan's metagenomics pipelines and biology work often require assembling long sequences.
  Sparse attention (e.g., sparse Transformer, Longformer) or linear RNNs could enable longer context for scientific
  queries without exceeding 32 GB.
- **LoRA-compatible methods**: Many sparse/linear variants are compatible with LoRA fine-tuning (sections 3.4, 4.4).
  Prioritize methods that don't require custom kernels (stick to MLX-native ops).
- **ANE utilization**: Quantized attention (4.4) and grouped attention (4.2) have simpler compute graphs, potentially
  amenable to ANE via pmetal.

## What's NOT applicable / hype filter

- Most benchmarks assume CUDA/A100; latency numbers don't transfer to Metal
- Methods requiring custom kernels (FlashAttention, streaming kernels) need MLX or pmetal equivalents
- Batch-size scaling experiments don't apply (Apple Silicon unified memory, no multi-GPU data parallelism)
- VRAM budgets (48 GB+) exceed Linus ceiling; must filter to sub-32 GB models

## Connections

This survey is the efficiency-axis companion to the broader architecture taxonomy in
[`2412.03220`](2412.03220.md) and the long-context survey in [`2311.12351`](2311.12351.md). Read together they cover
modern decoder-only LLMs from three angles: taxonomy, long-context techniques, and efficiency.

The linear-sequence and recurrent-resurgence thread is most directly continued by [`2410.01201v3`](2410.01201v3.md)
(minLSTM/minGRU — RNNs reorganized for parallel scan match Transformers on selective copy and small-LM benchmarks);
the BitNet thread ([`2310.11453v1`](2310.11453v1.md), [`2502.11880v1`](2502.11880v1.md),
[`2504.12285v2`](2504.12285v2.md)) attacks the orthogonal axis (bits per weight) but is also a Section-4-style
"efficient-attention via quantization" technique. The operations-level survey in [`2410.11381`](2410.11381.md)
identifies FFN as the dominant cost, which is consistent with this paper's MoE / sparse-FFN emphasis.

For Linus the survey's most actionable categories are linear-attention / SSM (Section 2 — Metal-friendly because no
full KV materialization) and quantized / IO-aware attention (Section 4 — composes with KV-cache compression for the
32 GB ceiling). The flash-moe study in
[`docs/syntheses/repo-clusters/g1-apple-silicon.md`](../syntheses/repo-clusters/g1-apple-silicon.md) is one concrete
empirical datapoint on what works and what doesn't on Apple Silicon — the Section 5 hybrid-architecture line is the
right place to come back to it.

The survey also implicitly motivates the memory-pillar choice in
[`docs/specs/memory-architecture.md`](../specs/memory-architecture.md): Layer A's substrate is whatever the dispatched
Worker provides, and survey Section 1's linear-recurrent variants are the natural Phase 6+ candidates if Linus ever
needs to dispatch a Worker with explicitly addressable hidden state. See [DEC-0042](../adr/0042-coconut-phase6-substrate-experiment.md)
for the related Coconut-as-substrate spike.

## Open questions for Dan

1. **MLX-native subset of the menu.** The survey catalogs ~300 methods, mostly with CUDA-side implementations.
   Which of the headline categories (linear attention, sparse attention, hybrid 50/50) actually have MLX-ready
   implementations Dan can pull and benchmark today, vs. require a port? A short scout of mlx-lm + Bonsai-demo +
   pmetal would establish the honest surface area.
2. **Hybrid-architecture timing for Phase 6.** The paper recommends 50% linear + 50% full attention as best
   practical throughput. For Linus's Phase 6 fine-tuning lane, is it premature to pursue hybrid architectures, or
   is there a tractable LoRA-on-hybrid-base experiment that would generate transferable info?
3. **MoE deferral, again.** Both this survey and [`2412.03220`](2412.03220.md) flag MoE as the scaling frontier.
   For 32 GB unified memory, is sparse-MoE (conditional activation of expert subsets) more attractive than dense
   7-13B, or is the routing overhead a deal-breaker on Apple Silicon? Worth probing once a BitNet-MoE GGUF lands.
4. **Diffusion-LLM watch flag.** The survey carves out non-autoregressive / diffusion LLMs. Currently this is
   speculative for Linus (diffusion LMs are mostly research artifacts), but is it worth a quarterly check-in on
   the thread, or formally retired until Phase 8?
