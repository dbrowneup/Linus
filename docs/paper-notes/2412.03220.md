# Survey of different Large Language Model Architectures: Trends, Benchmarks, and Challenges

## TL;DR

Comprehensive taxonomy of LLM architectures spanning auto-encoding (BERT family), auto-regressive (GPT family), and sequence-to-sequence models. Catalogs architectural innovations (positional encoding, attention variants, normalization, FFN design), evaluation benchmarks (GLUE, MMLU, SuperGLUE), and training/deployment challenges. Broadest survey in this batch; essential for Linus Phase 2 architecture selection and Phase 3 benchmark definition.

## The problem (in plain language)

The LLM landscape has exploded: BERT, GPT, T5, Llama, Gemma, Qwen, Mistral, Phi, and variants with proprietary tweaks. Each paper claims improvements; few compare apples-to-apples. This survey collects the genealogy, compares them on standard benchmarks, and identifies the fundamental trade-offs (encoding direction, generation strategy, parameter count, data efficiency).

## What they propose

Seven major architectural families:

1. **Auto-Encoding (bidirectional)**: BERT, RoBERTa, ALBERT, ERNIE. Masked language modeling. Best for understanding/classification. Cannot generate left-to-right.
2. **Auto-Regressive (unidirectional)**: GPT (1, 2, 3), LLaMA, Mistral, Qwen. Causal masking. Pure generation. Requires decoding strategy (greedy, beam search, nucleus sampling).
3. **Sequence-to-Sequence**: T5, mT5, mBART, Pangu. Encoder-decoder. Flexible for translation, summarization, QA. Requires finetuning for generation quality.
4. **Variational Auto-Encoders (VAE)**: Learn latent distributions. Data augmentation, anomaly detection. Not mainstream for LLMs.
5. **Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs)**: Generator + discriminator. Stable training hard; rare in modern LLMs.
6. **Pre-trained Language Models**: GPT-1 (base), BERT (masked MLM), GPT-2 (scaled causal). Foundational work.
7. **Modern Variants**: Grouped-query attention (GQA), flash attention, ALiBi, SwiGLU, mixture-of-experts, continued pretraining objectives.

Benchmarks: GLUE (understanding), SQuAD (reading comprehension), MMLU (knowledge), HumanEval (coding), HELM (comprehensive).

## Key results

- **Model size trends**: 1.3B (BERT) → 175B (GPT-3) → 65-70B (Llama 2) → 8-14B sweet spot for efficiency. Smaller models (7B Mistral, Phi-2) achieve near-GPT-3 performance with better latency/memory.
- **Attention scaling**: Multi-head attention (MHA) standard; grouped-query (GQA) reduces KV cache 8x with <1% accuracy loss. Sparse attention useful for very long sequences (>10k tokens).
- **Positional embeddings**: Rope (rotary) generalizes to 4x+ longer sequences than training. ALiBi competitive; does not require retraining for longer sequences.
- **Normalization**: LayerNorm vs. RMSNorm performance similar; RMSNorm computationally cheaper. Pre-norm (norm before FFN) more stable than post-norm (norm after).
- **FFN design**: SwiGLU, GeGLU, GELU gates improve training dynamics. Inference latency similar to simpler activations (ReLU); choice driven by training stability.
- **Data efficiency**: Recent small models (Phi-2, Mistral, Gemma-2B) match GPT-3 quality with 50-100x fewer parameters, suggesting architectural innovations (flash attention, better scaling laws) matter more than size.

## What's reusable in Linus

**Phase 2 (Immediate—architecture selection):**
- Decoder-only causal model (Llama/Mistral family) is the right choice for Linus Worker inference. Encoder-only (BERT) is for encoding Dan's KnowledgeBase, not for multi-turn reasoning.
- 7-13B model size: sweet spot for M1 Max unified memory (32 GB). Paper validates 7B Mistral ≈ 13B Llama in quality. Start with 7B Mistral 7B or Llama 2 7B.
- GQA as default: all modern models adopt it. KV cache savings essential for Linus.
- Rope positional embeddings: enables 4x+ sequence length without retraining. Use for long-context KnowledgeBase integration.
- Flash attention: if implementing custom Metal kernels, prioritize flash attention for latency/memory efficiency.

**Phase 3 (Knowledge integration):**
- Dual-path architecture: small encoder (BERT-style, 110M) for fast document embedding; larger decoder (7B) for reasoning. Applicable to KnowledgeBase RAG pipeline.
- Mixture-of-experts (MoE): paper flags MoE as emerging trend for scaling without dense compute. Defer to Phase 4; not applicable at 7-13B scale on single device.

**Phase 5 (Interface refinement):**
- Benchmark suite: MMLU, HumanEval, SQuAD adapted for Dan's domain tasks. Paper's benchmark taxonomy is template for Linus evaluation harness.

**Phase 6 (Fine-tuning):**
- LoRA on 7-13B models is tractable within 32 GB. Paper validates 7B models reach GPT-3 quality with careful tuning; LoRA reduces memory 10-100x further.
- Instruction-tuning objectives (masked next-token prediction + causal LM) improve few-shot capability. Relevant for Linus fine-tuning on Dan's research notes.

## What's NOT applicable / hype filter

- **Scaling laws for 70B+ models**: Linus is capped at 13-14B by memory. Paper's findings on 40B+, 65B, 175B models (parameter efficiency, emergent abilities) extrapolate poorly to smaller scales.
- **Multi-modal (vision + language)**: Survey includes vision transformers (ViT), CLIP, Flamingo. Linus Phase 0-6 scope is text + code. Defer to Phase 8 (Vision Pro).
- **Distributed training**: Multi-GPU optimizations (tensor parallelism, pipeline parallelism, FSDP) not applicable to single M1 Max.
- **Enterprise scale**: Survey discusses data center deployment, cost/latency trade-offs at scale. Linus is single-machine; those concerns irrelevant.
- **Reinforcement learning from human feedback (RLHF)**: Paper mentions RLHF but does not detail it. Implementing RLHF on 32 GB is an open problem; defer to Phase 7+.

## Open questions for Dan

1. **Worker base-model lock-in for Phase 2.** The survey makes a strong case for "7-13B decoder-only with GQA + RoPE
   + RMSNorm + SwiGLU" as the modern default. For Linus's Phase 2 Worker stable, is the right move to commit to one
   base family (Llama 3 / Mistral / Qwen) and stick with it through Phase 6 fine-tuning, or to keep the dispatcher
   model-agnostic and let the router primitives in DEC-0031 pick per-task?
2. **Encoder-only role for KnowledgeBase.** The survey separates auto-encoding (BERT family) from auto-regressive
   uses cleanly. KnowledgeBase per DEC-0015 is dual-substrate; should the embedding side commit to a small BERT-class
   encoder (110M-class) for retrieval, or use a frozen decoder's hidden states? The trade-off is latency vs.
   retrieval quality, and the survey doesn't resolve it.
3. **MoE deferral question.** The survey flags MoE as the scaling frontier. With BitNet-MoE variants on the horizon
   (referenced in [`2502.11880v1`](2502.11880v1.md)), should Phase 6's fine-tuning lane explicitly carve out an
   MoE track, or is it safe to keep MoE Phase 8+ research and stay dense for now?
4. **Benchmark template adoption.** The survey's HELM/MMLU/HumanEval/GLUE coverage is GPU-server-shaped, not
   Dan-task-shaped. Is there value in lifting only the structure (per-skill probe + comprehensive aggregate) into
   `benchmarks/dan_tasks/` while substituting Dan-relevant content, or is that already implicit in the existing
   benchmark scaffold?
