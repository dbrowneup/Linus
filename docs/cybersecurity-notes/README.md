# docs/cybersecurity-notes/

Government and standards primers relevant to the cybersecurity surface of a personal AI assistant operated by a
working scientist in biotech / genomics. Treat these as background reading for the threat-modelling work in
[`../syntheses/security-synthesis.md`](../syntheses/security-synthesis.md) and the SAFETY policy in
[`../../SAFETY.md`](../../SAFETY.md).

## Notes

- `01-NIST-Framework-v1.1.md` — NIST Cybersecurity Framework, the structuring vocabulary for everything below.
- `02-NIST-SP-800-207-ZeroTrust.md` — zero-trust architecture, especially the implicit-trust assumption that local-
  primary AI needs to reject.
- `03-NIST-SP-800-171r2-CUI.md` — Controlled Unclassified Information handling, relevant to research data on a
  personal device.
- `04-NCSC-China-Genomics.md` — NCSC analysis of the genomics threat surface (state-aligned data harvest of biological
  data).
- `05-HHS-Cyberthreats-Biotech.md` — HHS cyberthreats in biotech sector, the U.S. complement to the NCSC note.
- `06-Foley-Biotech-IP-Confidentiality.md` — Foley Hoag on biotech IP confidentiality, the practitioner view.
- `07-NCCoE-Genomics-Workshop.md` — NCCoE genomics-security workshop outputs.
- `08-NIST-SP-800-160v1.md` — Engineering trustworthy secure systems — the systems-engineering frame.
- `09-tomshardware-cursor-claude-incident.md` — a recent industry incident worth knowing.

See [`index.md`](index.md) for the formal table of contents.

## Source posture

These are excerpts and structured summaries of public-domain or fair-use academic / government material, captured here
so the security-synthesis can cite a single canonical local copy rather than re-fetching against possibly-moving URLs.
